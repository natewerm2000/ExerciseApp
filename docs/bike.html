Cardio Training

Bicycle

Bicycle Riding (outdoors)
Bicycle riding is another cardio workout similar to running averaging roughly 400 calories per hour with a plus of lower body strength from pedalling. If you want a gentle workout that is not as strenious on your joints and ankles, biking is for you. Its a great way to get the distance in and get outside. Starting as a biker, you should just flat roads/paths, if you are ready for a tougher workout, add some hills and distance in your biking session. It is recommended for the average biker to ride 30-60 minutes 3-5 days a week.

Stationary Bike (indoors)
Stationary bikes are a great indoor exercise, with very similar benefits to a regular bike. But with the added plus of it being in your home or at the gym. Owning a stationary bike at home means quick and easy workouts, in the comfort of your home without the stress of riding on the streets with possible threats of weather or vehicles. Compared to the usual cardio equipment, stationary bikes put the least amount of stress on your joints, while still providing an excellent cardio workout.

Bike Workouts
For beginners:

Low Intensity: 5-10 minutes

Medium intensity for 5 minutes followed by:

high intensity 1-2 minutes

medium intensity 5 minutes

high intensity 1-2 minutes

medium intensity 5 minutes

For weight loss:

Pedaling for 5 - 10 minutes at a low intensity

medium intensity 3-5 minutes

alternate: high(1-3 minutes) and medium (3-5 minutes) for the next 20 to 30 minutes

10 minute low intensity cooldown

For interval training

Start off low intensity for 10 minutes

medium intensity 10 minutes followed by:

high intensity: 2 minutes

low intensity: 2 minutes

high intensity: 2 minutes

low intensity: 2 minutes

high intensity: 2 minutes

10 minute cooldown
